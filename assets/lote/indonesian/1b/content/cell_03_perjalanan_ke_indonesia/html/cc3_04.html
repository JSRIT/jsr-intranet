Prepositions are words such as on top, below, beside, in front, behind, beneath.

Di is often used with a preposition to indicate physical location – in, on, or at.

di atas on top of / above di depan in front
di bawah underneath/below di belakang behind
di samping next to (di) dalam inside

This language learning strategy is called 'total physical response' and can be really useful for learning action words (such as throw and catch) and directions. Many people learn by doing, so try associating an action with a word and actually doing it while saying the word aloud. You will probably find you learn the words a lot faster.

Go through the kartu petunjuk and use a highlighter to highlight all the prepositions. For example:

Simpanlah barang Anda di tempat di atas tempat duduk.

Pada means in/at/on a time (for example in July, at twelve o'clock, on Tuesday). Sometimes you will see 'di' used for time also.

pada waktu

pada saat

Pada can also be used when you are instructing someone where to put something (don't put your baggage in the locker above your head), but this is less common.

You can also use sewaktu, selama or (apa)bila to say 'when' or 'whilst'.

sewaktu

selama

apabila is conditional – similar to 'if'. You may see it as just bila or bilamana.

Don't forget, the words sebelum and sesudah/setelah are also used to indicate time.

Go through the kartu petunjuk with a different coloured highlighter and highlight all the time indicators.

Verbs are going to be very prominent as key words in instructions. Verbs can take a number of different forms. You can often recognise verbs by their affixes.

  • Me- and di- are both verb prefixes, as is ber-.

    Can you work out the difference between the me- and the di- verbs in the sentences below?

    Saya mematikan lampu.

    Lampu dimatikan.

  • Sometimes prefixes are dropped, but you can still recognise a verb by its suffix. For example, -kan and -i are both verb suffixes.

  • Sometimes verbs appear in their base-word form only.

  • -lah can also be attached to an action word to make it an instruction. Jangan is not technically a verb, but -lah is sometimes attached to it to soften the instruction.

Revise me- verbs in the pojok bahasa section. Then, practise the different verb forms from the plane's kartu petunjuk by completing the worksheet. It may also help to revise your dictionary skills in the strategi section.

Finally, go through the kartu petunjuk and highlight all the verbs.

You should now be ready to do your English translation of the kartu petunjuk, but it will help to read about WC in the pojok budaya first.
